Hecker (Caracal)

This medium-sized wildcat can be found in sections of the Middle East, Africa's savannas, and forests. Typically, its coat is tawny or reddish gold with white patches on the chin, throat, and underside. Caracals hunt anything they can find, including birds, rodents, mongooses, hyraxes, and even tiny monkeys. They are opportunistic predators.

Beluga (Domestic Short-haired Cat)

Domestics are frequently gregarious beings but can also be lively, friendly, quiet, talkative, docile, or serene. Because of her endurance abilities, like as balancing and leaping, as well as her excellent hunting instinct, the Domestic Shorthair is regarded as a working cat. Her jovial nature frequently reflects these traits. She is not naturally violent, which makes her a wonderful friend for kids and elderly. With an appropriate introduction period, she also makes a wonderful playmate for other cats, dogs, and animals.

Smudge Cat (Van Cat)

The Turkish Van cat is a big, active cat that has no inherited propensity for illnesses. She exhibits her physical prowess in both the sea and on land. The Turkish Van cat is a powerful and skilled swimmer, which has earned this breed the moniker "The Swimming Cat." This is one feature of the Turkish Van cat nature that makes her so distinctive.
